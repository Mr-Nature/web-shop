Thai herbal tea, also known as "ชาสมุนไพร" (pronounced as "Cha Samunphrai" in Thai), has a rich history deeply rooted in Thailand's traditional medicine and cultural heritage.

The consumption of herbal teas in Thailand dates back centuries and is an essential part of Thai folk remedies.

The preparation of Thai herbal tea usually involves boiling the herbs and spices in water and sometimes sweetening them with sugar or honey to taste.

The resulting brew is often served hot, although it can also be enjoyed cold, especially in hot climate regions.
